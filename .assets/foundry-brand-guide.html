Part I

Brand Foundation

Before colors and fonts, there's philosophy. This section is the north star — everything else in this guide flows from these decisions.

Brand Essence

Helping people become more fully alive through thoughtfully leveraging technology.

We exist because technology should serve human flourishing — not the other way around. We're a craft-oriented consultancy that helps business owners build systems, automations, and AI integrations with intention, so they can focus on the work and life that matters most.

Tagline

Thoughtful technology craftsmanship.

"Thoughtful" speaks to intention and care. "Technology" is our medium. "Craftsmanship" signals that we approach our work the way an artisan approaches theirs — with patience, skill, and pride in the outcome.

Brand Qualities

This, not that.

These paired qualities define our personality. The first word is who we are. The second is the territory we deliberately avoid. Together, they create a corridor — wide enough to be expressive, narrow enough to be recognizable.

Curious, not formal.

We lead with genuine interest, not polished corporate distance. We ask good questions. We admit when we don't know. Our expertise shows through our curiosity, not our credentials.

Calm, not hectic.

In a world of urgency theater, we're the steady hand. Our communication, our process, and our design all carry an unhurried confidence. We create breathing room, not anxiety.

Measured, not rigid.

We're intentional without being inflexible. We have strong principles held lightly. We plan thoughtfully and adapt readily. Structure serves us — we never serve structure.

Human, not machine.

We build technology for people, and we never forget that. Emotions are a feature, not a bug. Every system we design has human flourishing as its success metric.

Values

What we stand on.

These aren't aspirational — they're operational. Every decision we make, every piece of content we create, every system we build should be traceable back to one of these.

Authenticity

Say what we mean and mean what we say. We believe in values-based living — and that starts with being honest about who we are, what we can do, and what we can't.

Craft

Centered on people and timeless values, not technology and trends. We believe emotions are a feature, not a bug, and that technology is an accelerator — powerful when it amplifies what's already good, dangerous when it replaces what's deeply human.

Curiosity

Grounded in intellectual humility and constant growth. We don't pretend to have all the answers. We ask better questions, stay hungry to learn, and treat every engagement as a chance to understand something new.

Clarity

Writing is our superpower. We believe in curation and distillation, radical simplicity, and the conviction that focus is everything. If we can't explain it clearly, we don't understand it well enough.

Unhurried

Work in wise, restful rhythms. We believe in oscillation — seasons of intensity balanced by seasons of rest. In gradual change over dramatic overhaul. In pushing over the next domino rather than trying to knock them all down at once.

Action orientation doesn't mean speed. It means doing the right next thing, at the right pace.

Positioning & ICP

Who we serve, and why us.

The Automation Foundry is a craft-oriented technology consultancy for values-driven business owners who know that investing in the right systems is wise — and that hiring a specialist to build them is wiser.

Our ideal client

Solo operators through small business owners (1–25 people), across industries. What unites them isn't sector — it's disposition. They value quality over speed, people over metrics, and long-term partnership over quick transactions. They understand that great systems free them to be in their sweet spot.

What we do

Four interconnected services: systems and automation consulting (strategy and implementation), AI integration and workflow optimization, fractional CTO and technology advisory, and training and education through guides, workshops, and deep-dive resources.

Our model

We openly share high-clarity, high-value information as a qualifier and trust builder — through a public manifesto, educational content, frameworks, and interactive resources. This isn't a lead magnet strategy. It's a genuine commitment to transparency and generosity. The right people see themselves in our thinking and want to go deeper. The wrong-fit people self-select out early, which respects everyone's time.

Visual Identity

Our visual system is built to feel like the brand it represents: warm but not saccharine, precise but not cold, distinctive but not loud.

Proportion — the 60/30/10 rule

Limestone dominates as the background canvas (60%). Current appears in navigation, headings, cards, and supporting elements (30%). Copper is used sparingly — for CTAs, links, and moments of warmth (10%). Mist and Ink serve structural roles: borders, dividers, body text, and dark-mode surfaces.

Dark mode

In dark mode, Ink becomes the background surface. Body text shifts to Limestone at roughly 87% opacity. Current and Copper remain consistent — they're the thread of identity connecting both modes. Never invert colors directly; adjust for the context while preserving the personality.

Accessibility

Ink on Limestone passes WCAG AA at 12.4:1 contrast. Current on Limestone passes at 4.6:1 (sufficient for large text and UI components). Copper on Limestone achieves 3.6:1 — use at 18px or larger, or pair with Ink for smaller text. Always test pairings.

Typography

Literata meets Manrope.

A humanist reading serif paired with a geometric sans-serif. Literata brings warmth, craft, and quiet authority to headings. Manrope brings clarity and modern legibility to body copy and UI. Together: craft meets technology.

Weights & spacing

Three weights per typeface. Literata: Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500). Manrope: Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500). Avoid bold — confidence comes from hierarchy, not heaviness. Body text at default tracking. Headings 32px+ get -0.01em. All-caps eyebrows get +0.08 to +0.12em.

Fallback chain

Literata → Georgia → "Times New Roman" → serif. Manrope → "Helvetica Neue" → Helvetica → Arial → sans-serif.

Logo System

The mark and the lockup.

Two overlapping circles represent the intersection of human need and technological capability. The hatched intersection uses Current on light backgrounds and Copper on dark — a subtle but meaningful shift that connects the logo to the full palette system.

Clear space & minimum size

Maintain clear space equal to the radius of one circle on all sides. Full lockup minimum: 180px wide (digital) or 1.5" (print). Icon minimum: 32px (digital) or 0.4" (print).

What not to do

Don't stretch, rotate, or recolor outside approved variants. Don't add shadows, gradients, or outlines. Don't place on busy backgrounds without contrast. Don't separate elements from the lockup. Don't use the badge element independently.

Imagery Direction

Three layers, one visual world.

Our imagery system uses three distinct approaches, each with a clear role. Together they create a visual language that feels handmade, intentional, and unmistakably ours.

Primary — Identity

Watercolor washes

Abstract watercolor textures in palette colors — muted blues, warm coppers, soft limestone. Used for hero backgrounds, section dividers, and accent elements. This is what makes us visually unmistakable. The organic, handmade quality that separates us from every tech company with a gradient.

Secondary — Clarity

Abstract line art

Minimal, geometric line illustrations for diagrams, process flows, and icons. Fine strokes in Current or Ink on light backgrounds, Limestone or Copper on dark. Structured enough to feel precise, hand-drawn enough to feel human.

Tertiary — Proof

Monochromatic photography

High-contrast, warm-toned black and white. Used sparingly for case studies, team imagery, workspace shots. Always candid, never posed. Warm highlights, lifted shadows. Shows the real — people, spaces, work in progress.

Art direction notes

Natural light, warm tones, shallow depth of field. Show hands at work. Calm, focused expressions. Clean but lived-in environments. No stock photography, no corporate settings, no forced smiles, no people pointing at screens.

Part III

Voice & Writing

For a consulting company, written communication is the primary brand touchpoint. Every email, proposal, and blog post — that's where people experience who we are. Voice is personality (constant). Tone is mood (variable).

Voice Pillars

Four commitments, always on.

Plainspoken Expertise

We explain complex technology clearly. We're the translator, not the lecturer. If jargon doesn't serve the reader, we don't use it. We earn trust by making things understandable.

Thoughtful Intentionality

Every word has purpose. We don't rush, pad, or hype. Short sentences for impact, longer ones for nuance. White space on the page reflects white space in the mind.

Warm Confidence

Certain of our craft without arrogance. "Here's what we've found" rather than "As industry experts." Our confidence is quiet — it doesn't need to announce itself.

Honest Partnership

We speak directly and acknowledge complexity. We don't oversimplify, and we don't pretend every problem has a clean solution. When we don't know, we say so.

Tone Adaptation

Same voice, different rooms.

Our voice stays constant. Tone shifts by context — like a real person adapts their energy while staying authentically themselves.

By context

Onboarding a client: Warm, welcoming, unhurried.

Delivering a project: Confident, clear, measured pride.

Explaining a problem: Direct, calm, solution-oriented.

Educational content: Curious, exploratory, generous.

What this sounds like

A project update email, written three ways. The first two are wrong. The third is us.

✕ Too corporate
"We are pleased to inform you of significant progress across all deliverables. The technical implementation phase has been completed per the agreed-upon specification."
✕ Too casual
"Hey! Tons of awesome stuff happened this week!! We crushed the API integration and everything's looking great! 🎉"
✓ On brand
"Good progress this week. We completed the API integration and ran initial tests — everything's working as expected. A few things worth discussing before the next phase."

Writing Principles

How we write.

Use active voice

"We built the integration" not "The integration was built." Active voice is clearer, shorter, and more honest about who did what.

Use contractions

"We're" not "We are." Contractions make writing sound like speech, which is how we want to sound — like a real person talking to another real person.

Lead with what matters to the reader

Don't make people dig for the point. Open with the outcome, then provide context. Respect their time.

Vary sentence rhythm

Short sentences create impact. Longer sentences carry nuance. Mix them. Read aloud — if you can't breathe naturally, restructure.

Prefer short paragraphs

One idea per paragraph. Three to four sentences maximum. White space is thinking space.

Show, don't just tell

Instead of saying "we're experts," show the thinking. Let the quality of the writing be the evidence for the quality of the work.

Part IV

Our Principles

These are meant to be shared publicly — both as a statement of who we are and as a filter for who we work with. The right people will see themselves in these words. That's the point.

The Automation Foundry Manifesto — Draft

What we believe about technology and being human.

Technology should make you more fully alive — not more fully busy.

The measure of a good system isn't how much it can do. It's how much space it creates for the things that matter — deep work, real relationships, rest, and the creative pursuits that make you feel like yourself.

Emotions are a feature, not a bug.

Frustration with your tools is data. Excitement about a new possibility is signal. The way technology makes you feel isn't secondary to what it does — it's part of the outcome. We design for the whole human.

The best systems are built at a human pace.

Rushing creates technical debt and relational debt in equal measure. A little bit at a time, consistently, beats everything.

Clarity is kindness.

If we can't explain it simply, we don't understand it well enough. Clear writing, clear systems, and clear communication are acts of respect for the people on the other end.

People over tools. Always.

We're centered on timeless values, not trending technology. Tools change constantly. The humans using them — their needs, their capacity for growth — those are what we build around.

Generosity is a business model.

We share what we know openly, not as a tactic, but because transparency builds the kind of trust that makes good work possible. The right relationships start with giving.

Do the right thing, even when it's the slow thing.

We value quality over speed, integrity over efficiency, and doing what's right for people over what's merely expedient. Not every system should be automated. Part of our job is knowing the difference.

This manifesto is a draft, meant to be refined through use and conversation. The deeper "why" essay behind these principles is a future piece — these seven points are the distilled version that can stand alone on a website page or print piece.
