# Voice

**[VERIFIED]** — extracted from `.assets/foundry-brand-guide.html`. For a consulting company,
written communication IS the primary brand touchpoint: every email, proposal, and blog post is
where people experience who Foundry is.

**Voice is personality (constant). Tone is mood (variable).** Everything below describes voice
first, then how tone flexes by context without breaking it.

## Four voice pillars — always on

**Plainspoken Expertise** — Explain complex technology clearly. Be the translator, not the
lecturer. If jargon doesn't serve the reader, don't use it. Trust is earned by making things
understandable, not by demonstrating vocabulary.

**Thoughtful Intentionality** — Every word has purpose. Don't rush, pad, or hype. Short sentences
for impact, longer ones for nuance. White space on the page reflects white space in the mind.

**Warm Confidence** — Certain of the craft, without arrogance. "Here's what we've found" rather
than "As industry experts." The confidence is quiet — it doesn't need to announce itself.

**Honest Partnership** — Speak directly and acknowledge complexity. Don't oversimplify, and don't
pretend every problem has a clean solution. When something isn't known, say so.

## Tone by context

Voice stays constant; tone shifts like a real person adapting their energy while staying
themselves.

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Onboarding a client | Warm, welcoming, unhurried |
| Delivering a project | Confident, clear, measured pride |
| Explaining a problem | Direct, calm, solution-oriented |
| Educational content | Curious, exploratory, generous |

## The same message, three ways

A project-update email, written three ways. The first two are wrong. The third is Foundry.

**✕ Too corporate:** "We are pleased to inform you of significant progress across all
deliverables. The technical implementation phase has been completed per the agreed-upon
specification."

**✕ Too casual:** "Hey! Tons of awesome stuff happened this week!! We crushed the API integration
and everything's looking great! 🎉"

**✓ On brand:** "Good progress this week. We completed the API integration and ran initial tests
— everything's working as expected. A few things worth discussing before the next phase."

Notice what the on-brand version does: leads with the takeaway, states facts plainly, flags what
still needs a decision — without hype or stiffness.

## Writing principles

- **Use active voice.** "We built the integration," not "The integration was built." Clearer,
  shorter, more honest about who did what.
- **Use contractions.** "We're," not "We are." Writing should sound like speech — a real person
  talking to another real person.
- **Lead with what matters to the reader.** Don't make people dig for the point. Open with the
  outcome, then provide context. Respect their time.
- **Vary sentence rhythm.** Short sentences create impact. Longer sentences carry nuance. Mix
  them. Read aloud — if you can't breathe naturally, restructure.
- **Prefer short paragraphs.** One idea per paragraph, three to four sentences max. White space
  is thinking space.
- **Show, don't just tell.** Instead of asserting "we're experts," show the thinking. Let the
  quality of the writing be the evidence for the quality of the work.

## Word calibration

Not exhaustive — a calibration tool. When in doubt: *would I say this across the table to
someone I respect?*

**Words Foundry uses:** thoughtful · craft · build · understand · explore · clarity · intention ·
rhythm · care · partnership · steady · honest · human · purpose · simplify · refine · curious ·
flourish

**Words Foundry avoids:** leverage (as a verb) · synergy · disrupt · cutting-edge · revolutionary
· game-changing · scalable · best-in-class · seamless · turnkey · robust · utilize · paradigm ·
ecosystem · hack · crush it

## Counter-examples

| Don't write | Write instead |
|---|---|
| "We are pleased to announce our enhanced communication strategy framework." | "Here's a better way to think about this." |
| "Stakeholders should leverage the platform to optimize engagement." | "Use this. It works." |
| "Our innovative, cutting-edge solutions deliver value." | (Delete the sentence entirely.) |
| "It is recommended that organizations consider implementing…" | "We think you should try…" |

## When in doubt

Read it aloud. If it sounds like a memo, rewrite it. If it sounds like a Foundry person talking
to a peer they respect, ship it.

See `principles.md` for the values and manifesto this voice is downstream of — the voice isn't
arbitrary style, it's what "clarity is kindness" and "people over tools" sound like on the page.
